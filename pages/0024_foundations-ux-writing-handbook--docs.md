Principles

Our mission is to give everyone the power to build and improve how their organization runs.
With monday.com, anyone can easily create or customize software that suits their needs.

Clarity

Simple language - use language that’s easy to understand. Look for plain-language alternatives to technical terms and jargon, and give explanations when there are no alternatives.

Concision - keep messages short and direct, and eliminate any unnecessary information.
Consistency
Terminology - use approved and consistent terminology whenever you communicate with users.

Formatting - use a consistent format for similar messages (error notifications, for example) across all features and products.

Accessibility

Inclusive language - write respectfully for diverse audiences. Use gender-neutral terms and avoid exclusive language.

Readability - write short sentences with familiar terms to keep your content readable.
Personality

If monday.com was a person, it would be the ultimate assistant.

Expert and reliable

We’re an approachable partner with deep knowledge. We know what we’re doing, and we communicate with reassuring confidence. We own our mistakes and show how we’ll fix them.

Proactive and supportive

We go out of our way to help users, so they can trust us completely. We anticipate their needs and provide thoughtful solutions before they even ask.

Voice

Our voice is the consistent piece of how we communicate with users. It’s the style and emotional approach we always use while writing.

Empathetic and conversational

Our voice is “human to human”. We speak with users honestly and directly to develop an emotional connection with them.

Positive and professional

We use affirmative language to motivate users. We encourage them to get the most out of monday.com, and we support them in doing their best work.

Tone

While our voice is consistent, our tone exists on a spectrum. It’s emotionally nuanced, and it adapts to different situations.

Light and engaging

We gravitate toward a light and engaging tone for experiences where our message is simple and we have an opportunity to delight users, like new feature announcements or empty states.

Professional and reliable

We use a professional and reliable tone for experiences where our top priority is to reassure users that they’re in good hands, like system errors or anything involving payment.

Values & Golden Rules

If our voice and tone are how we write, then our values are why we write in that way. At the most practical level, our golden rules help us write consistently every time.

UX writing values
We work for the users
And they don’t work for us. That means we write copy that’s simple, intuitive, and relevant.
Stay consistent
Use consistent terminology across all features and products.
Keep it stress-free
Do not manipulate or guilt users, even if they choose to do something less profitable for you.
Gradual complexity
Introduce new features one step at a time. Learning takes time.
The pen is mightier than the sword
Do not use passive-aggressive or offensive language.
Write for all readers
Use clear language without technical jargon or narrow cultural references.
Golden rules of UX writing
Bottom line first
Start every message with the most important information.
Give full context
Provide enough context so users know exactly where they are.
Focus on the user's point of view
Focus on what the user wants to do and what they stand to gain, not on your own perspective.
One CTA
Present only one CTA at a time. It makes decisions easier and faster.
Use specific CTAs
Ensure that CTAs respond directly to the header.
Cut the fluff
Write briefly and to the point.
